THIS IS A REALLY FUN QUESTION FOR ME!!! midori's mental state before, during, and after repayment festival are actually so integral to his relationship with chiaki and answer a lot about why he acts a certain way (particularly his actions in !! era)

i talked about it before in a thread lovemailing the beauty of zodiac midori leading up to the current midori. i recommend reading it because i think it answers your question !! this time around, i'll break the thread down even further so it's (hopefully) easier to make sense of what i meant. h-hopefully

DISCLAIMER THOUGH THAT what i've written here is just a simplified summary. each segment has a lot of layers and depth to it... so there are definitely more nuances to it than what i've written here!

yeah, midori is pretty complex because he went through various stages with chiaki... all of which are interesting and important. SOOOOOOO IMPORTANT to the point that if you alter any of them their relationship just wouldn't work.

when they first meet, midori starts off feeling like chiaki is an overwhelming, confusing, obnoxious and suffocating person who just won't leave him alone and he can't for the life of him understand Why. no matter how many times he tries to escape or push chiaki away, chiaki just comes back stronger and more persistent. midori attempting to skip practice so much is WHY chiaki started to pick him up in the first place. (something midori never asked for! among many other things he never asked chiaki to do, ever)

we don't really get that many glimpses of this anxiety in stories, but climax reveals to us that a chunk of his behavior with ryuseitai and especially chiaki is the fear of not living up to people's expectations of him. that people expect something amazing and wonderful from midori because of his looks, get to know who he truly is, find out he's "nothing special", and get severely disappointed and leave him. experiencing that had really warped midori's perception of himself (thinking he TRULY has NOTHING in him and that nobody can change that "fact"), and made him believe only his family would ever love him for who he truly is deep down.

so imagine that midori, who came out of all those experiences back in middle school, immediately being bombarded with this relentless senior who's convinced there's something in him, that "he has the qualities to be a hero"... it's terrifying because how the hell would he know that???

but despite midori's attempts to escape or his whines, chiaki continued to pull him back in and get him to "show his potential". and this meant day in and out, midori would experience the shenanigans and the "mess" that is ryuseitai, this unit that barely takes itself seriously and yet apparently totally does because "we're heroes!"

as much as it exhausted him, he eventually gave in to it because it's "too late to quit this school now", "i don't really have much of a choice", etc... and that constant persistence to be pulled back into the light and warmth of chiaki and ryuseitai eventually became the norm for midori. this is where hero show, supernova, and plenty of other summer stories happen. supernova in particular marks the point where midori TRULY realizes he's doing something he would've NEVER done if he didn't enter the idol course, and seeing his middle school classmate just being a "normal person like he once was" while he's on stage further amplified that realization... but it makes him realize that after all that exhaustion and hell practice he went through... he's actually happy here, with ryuseitai.

and from there on, this new norm becomes so commonplace for midori that it feels worse when it ISN'T him with ryuseitai (like in tanabata. he didn't enjoy practice with trickstar much, mostly because of subaru, and felt relief when chiaki and kanata were finally back. This Will Be Important)

HERE is where we enter midori's potential as a hero budding and taking form, slowly but surely. and this beautifully transitions to the stories that explore midori's feelings towards chiaki: sweet halloween and zodiac

before that, one thing i LOVE about the summer stories is that it's also the time midori is most annoyed and fed up with chiaki, usually expressing it in a "it's already hot as it is (during the summer weather), can you NOT make it worse". this makes the contrast between summer midori and autumn midori so cute, because he starts to warm up even MORE to ryuseitai, as it's no longer a season with exhausting hot weather to deal with on top of chiaki. summer was also a veeeery busy time for ryuseitai, and then they had a break from work that chiaki made sure to give them, and i feel like that also contributed to accelerating his warming up and his potential finally taking form after all the new experiences.

and so, this perfectly leads into sweet halloween, where midori learns something entirely different and opposite of what he's understood about chiaki so far. all this time he's grown so used to chiaki being overly energetic, overly loud, overly suffocating, etc... but in this case, he's quieter? he's fidgeting? he seems overwhelmed, to the point that he has no time to "pester" midori...? i find it very interesting that the reason midori picked up on chiaki acting weird was because chiaki's behavior lacked the traits that he kept seeing from him over and over again. it went against midori's norm. the norm that chiaki HIMSELF created by constantly committing his time and efforts to midori day after day. (this will Also be important for later)
