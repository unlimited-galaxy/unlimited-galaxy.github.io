After learning about Chiaki and Tetora, im curious to know how you would qualify Midori's and Kanata's perception of justice according to what we've seen in Tropical and Stella Maris ?
And with Shinobu's potential center looming over the horizon, any theory about his vision of justice ?

tweet link of the answer

it's not a weird question at all !! this is one of the Best parts about the reborn ryuseitai in that it's ALL about their own form of justice. and all about discussing and learning more about it!! IT'S SO MUCH FUN!!

personally i'm still in the process of dissecting and analyzing every aspect of it, so warning in advance that my analysis and thoughts are still incomplete

i haven't 100% finalized how i would express all of this in words, but for now this is how my mind breaks down the themes of what each ryuseitai represents:

RB (tetora):

came to life by fighting against an actual evil in reality. a lot of it is because tetora wanted to be worth something, and to actually put in effort in something all the way. he also wanted to save his juniors and get them out of such a dangerous organization. the theme of "evil is justice too"

RB is a lot about paving a new pathway of justice in something that isn't typically regarded as such (in this case, evil). and by doing so, tetora proves that justice can be achieved in all kinds of forms! he validates all forms of justice. it doesn't have to be in the traditional ideal hero way at all.

what i love about RB as well is that it supports ryuseitai's principles. take for example ryusei hanabi's "if you pray for the peace of the world, then even yesterdays enemies are members" line. i feel like RB perfectly represents that concept and proves it to be true, since they are "heroes dyed in evil"!! and i specifically mean ryuseitai rather than tokusatsu principles, since !! era ryuseitai is a lot about breaking free from the chains of tradition but still remaining as who they are and what they believe in. it really suits tetora, the person who has always taken ryuseitai's catchphrases to heart (he constantly says how much he believes in the "all for one and one for all" phrase)...

RG (midori):

no such thing as enemies here. it's all about being yourself and loving what you love. midori focuses a lot on creating a space where ryuseitai can relax (after all, there are no enemies to fight in the first place). performed specifically for the sake of saving his beloved mascots who comforted him for many years

yuruseitai is unconventionally heroic. it's heroic in its essence (believing in something and standing your ground for what you believe in) but not in its shape (there are no enemies to fight, no justice vs evil). it perfectly fits midori and how he doesn't care at all for tokusatsu and other traditionally hero things. midori as a person tends to be pretty pessimistically pragmatic, yet his ideal "hero" vision is a world where nobody struggles at all... it's so precious?? and since the song focuses so much on being yourself, untied by any conventions or expectations it's so... like gosh, it's the perfect answer/sequel to !! era ryuseitai's arc 🥺 reading tropical after watching relax paradise gives the impression that midori specifically wants this to be a relaxing place for his precious comrades the most, above all... not to mention its strong focus on being so goofy and silly and embracing that!!

RC (kanata):

after kanata rejected being a leader many times, his ryuseitai finally came to be due to his wish to repay everyone around him, since it's thanks to them that he's here. focuses on saving a boy who looked exactly like his young self, along with the island he's from. to make others experience the happiness he received from people

fish ryuseitai... i'm still in the middle of analyzing it so what i can say right now is limited, but it basically encourages an endless cycle of love and kindness. repaying kindness with kindness, love with love, until it spreads across the whole world and now everyone is smiling together... and that's how people's fears and anxieties about life, their future, their situation etc. fade away. it fits perfectly to kanata's backstory (the "evil things" that can negatively influence people, and can only be purified by someone like kanata). it's taking everything about kanata thus far and reshaping it into his ideal form of justice... idk it's so 🥺🥺🥺 especially with abyss in mind, knowing that from day 1 kanata has only ever wanted to dispel "evil", whether as an ally of justice or god.

aaand we've yet to know RR (chiaki) and RY (shinobu)

so with this in mind, so far we can say that the ryuseitais contain these elements:

1: some kind of inner, selfish wish

2: an ideal vision that's grounded in reality

3: there's some kind of specific, tangible target for it (whether to fight against or to save)

and this is what leads it to be a unique ryuseitai of its own. the contrasts between the ryuseitais are fascinating and so interesting to look into...

compare this all to ! era ryuseitai, that were a traditional justice fighting against traditional evil... and the evil would almost always be figurative or fictional, and it never actually "truly" existed. if it did, it was purely a skit. like a hero show or CGI (but disclaimer that this description is extremely oversimplified and i'd prob word it better with enough re-reading and research... it's also lacking the explanation to how ryuseitai came to be vs reborn ryuseitais so, yeah... this is just the gist of it!!)

i would say that nekketsu ryusei ninpouchou and suisei halation fit the above description a lot more than reborn ryuseitai... and for good reason! because it's not >their< unique ryuseitai yet, but rather the ryusetai we had known for many years. it's still a form of ryuseitai that's important and special though. and i actually find it really symbolic that it's shinobu and chiaki who got a center song pre-new ryuseitai formation, as those two uphold the "conventions" we've known about ryuseitai best. shinobu has always been the person who takes after chiaki's heroism most, especially because chiaki always took him and his ninja stuff seriously by actually finding ways to make practical use of it (like in orihime and hikoboshi, it's mentioned in living sunshine chapters)

that being said both songs still have a very "new era" vibe to them... i can't put it in words but they definitely make me think "these songs would've never existed in ! era". it shows their development and the evolution ryuseitai has been slowly going through since ! era days...

so in other words i want to wait to see what RR and RY end up being to talk about theirs... we've been getting a couple of hints here and there across many stories and i'm really curious what akira will reveal once their 2nd center comes around...

RY is a given, but RR will also definitely happen despite chiaki having been a leader for a long time already. this time around, his reborn ryuseitai should reflect who he truly is deep inside, rather than what he believed he had to be to become a hero (since chiaki's arc has a lot to do with how heroes never existed in reality, so he had to find a way to make it a reality at last, even if it cost him a lot of mental and physical energy.)

as for RY... akira has been telling us a lot about unmasked shinobu (i.e. doesn't use ninja speech, uses "boku" instead of "sessha") in various stories and "reminding" us that ninja is not the only part that makes shinobu... and i seriously don't know what he's cooking for us but it's gonna blow our minds for sure... i'm certain it'll be a SUPER interesting contrast to his 1st center !!!

again, sorry for such an incomplete, fragmented analysis....😭 with enough research and sourcing i think i could write a more precise analysis about all of it... i also still need to finish digesting stella maris some more... but this is the overall vibes i get from it so far!!! they're all beautiful and perfect in every way ❤️❤️ thank you for asking!!!
