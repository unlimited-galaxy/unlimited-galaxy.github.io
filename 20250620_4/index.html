I'M HAPPY TO HEAR THAT!! i love considering stuff based on canon, there is so much to work off of...

i know i took a long time to answer this, but the question is very interesting because something fun about midochia is that it's not really a topic of "do they love each other? do they want to be together forever?" but rather "do they realize they love each other in that way?" and it's even MORE interesting on midori's side.

in terms of "do midochia love each other?" it's yes, and "do they know they're loved by the other?" it's also yes. by the end of his first year, midori realizes chiaki's love for him is 100% legit and was never going to fade off once he "disappoints" chiaki, chiaki genuinely loves him deep down for who he is and that's why he wants to see him become a hero. on the other hand, comet show is when chiaki realizes he's loved by midori. he spent so many months, even back in ! era, possibly thinking midori doesn't care about him (and especially doesn't care about him as much as midori cares for the other members), but by the end of comet show he realizes once and for all, midori loves and cares for him so much.

"do they want to be together forever?" absolutely 100% yes, this is such a big theme of ryuseitai's arc in general, they all want to be together for as long as physically possible, and these two were at the center of that arc considering midori intentionally stopped chiaki from leaving ryuseitai. he did EVERYTHING in his power to make sure chiaki doesn't give up his hero dream

so what is so fascinating is... does midori realize so many of his actions toward chiaki are fueled by special love for chiaki? does midori realize he loves chiaki?

and i think the answer is yes and no. it's a bit complicated because midori is extremely layered. fortunately i've answered an ask somewhat related to this before right here, but it's 2 years old by now and i want to do a re-take on the whole topic (especially because a couple of my interpretations changed after so many more new stories came out), so this is an improved version of that ask!

THAT BEING SAID i absolutely love what anon said in that ask, so i do want to at least copy paste that again as part of this topic:

An interesting aspect of Midori's relationship with Chiaki that I enjoy thinking about is that for as much as Midori often tries to be straightforward and direct with Chiaki regarding how he feels (telling him to watch him closely, trying to tell him he won't think differently of him if he showed his weak side, etc) Midori also seems reluctant to admit those feelings at the same time? If anyone else brings up "oh Midori you're pretty close to Chiaki right? you miss him don't you?" his immediate response is to refute it and even occassionally feels violent over the idea. He seems incapable of realizing that "I always search what Morisawa-senpai is up to online and have memorized a bunch of stuff about him" isn't...ordinary, to put it nicely. Especially for someone he claims he's not obsessed with, he just wants to be sure Chiaki's not badmouthing him, honest! It's such a fun part of who Midori is. That he is both direct about how he truly feels about Chiaki and yet also very stubborn about not wanting to be honest about it. It seems contradictory and like it wouldn't work as a character trait for him, and yet the writing makes it so that both can be true and real and make sense for who Midori is as a person and how layered his relationship with Chiaki is.

YESS YES YES YES YES i have a personal interpretation of how he manages to be both at the same time.

firstly i agree, i love the seeming contradiction between the two traits, yet in reality it really gives depth to his character!

secondly, ABSOLUTELY YES, part of why he's like this is because he doesn't realize it. in regards to the egosearching, i feel like he doesn't fully understand that he's coping with the loss of chiaki, even though he's always talking about how he's so MIA now and it sucks. but also, he's trying his best to come to understand his feelings too!!

midori is straightforward with his feelings towards chiaki. when given the opportunity, he just expresses them as they are. those same feelings though, are intense and messy, and also come from all sorts of places. his emotional dependancy on chiaki that he's trying his best NOT to do anymore because of his desire to repay chiaki (to be reliable for him, to support him, to ease his burdens)... and then, his exhaustion being with an "energetic loud" chiaki, but also the attachment and endearment he has towards him existing in his life and being by his side... it goes on and on and on... every feeling has its own layer, and then that has its own layer, and it's so beautiful.

and that's something so important here: that there are many emotions and feelings he's going through. some of them he doesn't realize, some he hasn't fully understood, and some that he knows what it is and is trying his best to convey to chiaki. and he is CONSTANTLY trying to make sense of his emotions and get closer to achieving what he wants to do for chiaki (repay him, give him the same type of unconditional acceptance as chiaki did to him, etc.), but it takes SO LONG because HIS FEELINGS FOR CHIAKI ARE THAT MASSIVE. (also because chiaki has 39535 defensive walls that are keeping midori out but that's a different topic)

but... in terms of a label, what would you call these feelings? what on earth IS midori feeling for chiaki? he is literally feeling EVERY EMOTION EVER TO EXIST when coming into contact with chiaki. anger, happiness, sadness, confidence, irritation, infatuation, worry, relief... it's ENDLESS. no wonder midori doesn't know or doesn't realize what he's feeling towards chiaki. there's just too many to register at once in the first place

then someone sees him be that way and then says "oh, you really miss him, huh?" or "you love him so much huh~" or even chiaki himself says any of those things... and it angers him so much

here's where my own interpretation based on the text starts. because midori doesn't know what these feelings are, having people slap a label and move on, before he even gets to fully digest them, frustrates him.

it's like people are now designating how he feels. for one thing, a single-word label such as "love" doesn't even come close to describing his feelings, so i feel like it frustrates him to hear ppl summarize it as just "love". for another, we know from his stories that midori has always despised the image others have of him and place on him, he hates the expectation that comes with that image, for ex. "well midori is strong and handsome, so he must be very capable"... and he hates that it always leads to disappointment for "failing to meet" said expectations.

and for yet Another thing... midori's experience with love, especially romantic love, is complicated due to his bad experiences of girls being interested in him for his looks then getting disappointed when they find out his true self. this is another reason why i feel like "love" feels wrong in his mouth, as he's never even experienced the feelings of love and caring for others (outside of your family) before, not until ryuseitai. and he took a whole year of being with ryuseitai, and then being yelled at by tetora, to come to an understanding of his feelings towards ryuseitai. it's no surprise that it's taking him a long time to digest all these feelings he has for chiaki, considering it's on an even bigger and messier scale
